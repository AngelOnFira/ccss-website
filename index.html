Frequently Asked Questions

How do I join the Carleton Computer Science Society?

The Carleton Computer Science Society represents all students within Carleton's computer science program. That means that if you're in cs, you're already a member!

Will there be in-person events in the 2021-2022 academic year?

We will not be operating in-person during the Fall 2021 academic term. We will be evaluating if it is safe to host events in Winter 2022 later in the semester.

Is the CCSS an official Carleton club / society?

The CCSS is recognized and funded by the School of Computer Science, however it is not registered as a CUSA club.

Can I attend events if I'm not a computer science student?

Yes, unless otherwise specified. We occasionally have attendance restrictions when we host events that receive funding from the department which is directly taken from the tuition of computer science students.

How do I contact the executives?

Email info@ccss.carleton.ca to email all current executives. You can also find individual executive emails on our team page.

How is the board of directors chosen?

The board of directors are elected annually during the month of March. We hold first-year representative elections each September / October.

How can I get involved with the organization?

Checkout our volunteer page for more information! You can also run to become an executive during our annual general elections.

Does the CCSS have graduate students?

No, the CCSS represents all undergraduate Carleton computer science students. We do happily welcome graduate students to attend our events!
